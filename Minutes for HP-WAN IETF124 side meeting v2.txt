2. HP-WAN document updates (State of the Art and Framework)
   Presenters: Quan Xiong (10 min)

   Quan, Daniel King, and Daniel Huang provided an update on the two HP-WAN
   documents.

   Hiroshika: I also operate an audio-infrastructure research and education
   network. Have you studied current operations and traffic engineering in
   research and education networks?

   Daniel King: Yes. As described in the state-of-the-art document, we have
   authors who build and operate R&E networks. It reflects practice in North
   America, Europe, and two Chinese networks, and the tools are applied both
   offline and online. We would welcome a Japanese example and your scenarios.

   Hiroshika: The orchestration or controller work is good, but partnerships
   are based on MOUs across the community and depend on collaboration. I
   suggest studying Layer-3 operations and traffic engineering, and
   understanding mechanisms to control or align resources for activities.

   Daniel King: The networks are operated separately at Layer 3 with end-to-end
   services and connectivity. Orchestration, scheduling, and end-to-end
   signaling may be required.

4. PolKA
   Presenter: Magnos Martinello (20 min)

   Magnos Martinello: Introduced PolKA and discussed future work to help with
   HP-WAN.

   Tony: Trafino chipsets are no longer available. What can be used to access
   PolKA?

   Magnos: There is time to deploy into a final switch. There are other options, too.

   Daniel King: Would it be feasible to run path computation locally and
   translate it into whatever chipset instruction set is available in each
   forwarding domain? Do you see a hierarchical approach where one domain has
   P4 and another has a different chipset? It would need hardware-agnostic
   instructions.

   Magnos Martinello: It can work similarly to MPLS. There are gateways to
   translate and encapsulate from one domain to another, so the network can
   run PolKA natively.

   Daniel King: Assume we monitor application latency and bandwidth requirements.
   Endpoint application requests are a scenario in HP-WAN. We aim to standardise
   application-to-network interaction, either directly to a distributed control
   plane, via an intermediary device, or via an orchestrator/controller.

   Daniel King: Do you have plans for the Hackathon? Could the PolKA team
   contribute running code or input on the APIs used by your controller or
   orchestrator?

   Magnos Martinello: Not sure yet. We need to assess feasibility. We have
   several simulation environments.

   Daniel King: The Hackathon will happen at the next IETF. The purpose is to
   demonstrate implementations and collaborate on prototyping IETF work.
   Depending on the operator, this may be a use case or a potential architecture.
   IETF has building blocks that can be applied. We want to present a clear use
   case and demonstrate HP-WAN objective functions. There are third-party and
   open-source tools. We aim to publish everything as part of the Hackathon and
   integrate to show this.
